About AgentSLR

AgentSLR is a collaboration between the Reasoning with Machines Lab (OxRML) at the University of Oxford and the Pathogen Epidemiology Review Group (PERG) at the MRC Centre for Global Infectious Disease Analysis, Imperial College London.

The project brings together epidemiologists, computer scientists, and AI researchers to tackle one of the most resource-intensive tasks in evidence-based public health: systematic literature reviews.

Systematic reviews are essential for synthesising scientific evidence, yet they are costly, difficult to scale, and extremely time-intensive. AgentSLR automates the complete workflow — from article retrieval and multi-stage screening, through structured data extraction, to evidence synthesis via written reports.

Applied to epidemiological reviews of nine WHO-designated priority pathogens and validated against expert-curated ground truth, AgentSLR achieves screening decisions and data extractions consistent with human annotators while completing this process 58× faster (reducing review time from ~7 weeks to 20 hours).

The pipeline is open-source and open-weight, enabling reproducible and scalable evidence synthesis for outbreak preparedness and response.

Article Search & Retrieval

Data Sources: OpenAlex (30 req/s), PubMed (standard API), Europe PMC (20 req/s)

Search Strategy: Boolean queries with epidemiological terms, wildcard expansion for term variants, pathogen-specific exclusion criteria.

Process: Deduplication by DOI/PMID/PMCID, cascading PDF retrieval from open access sources, parallel downloads with rate limiting.

Example Query: [PATHOGEN] AND (transmissi* OR epidemiolog* OR model* OR severity OR "case fatality ratio*" OR "serial interval*" OR "reproduction number*" OR outbreak* ...)
b

Title & Abstract Screening

0%

Title & Abstract Screening

Inclusion Criteria:

  • English-language peer-reviewed research
  • Contains quantitative outbreak data
  • Reports transmission models or epidemiological parameters
  • Estimates key metrics (R0, serial interval, CFR, etc.)

Exclusion Criteria:

  • Conference proceedings, abstracts only
  • In-vitro studies without human/animal data
  • Case studies with <10 cases
c

PDF-to-Markdown Conversion

0%

PDF-to-Markdown Conversion

OCR Pipeline: Page-by-page rendering to high-resolution images, Mistral OCR for text extraction, preserves document hierarchy.

Output Features: LaTeX equations preserved, HTML tables maintained, Markdown formatting.

d

Full-text Screening

0%

Full-text Screening

Additional Requirements: Must contain extractable models or parameters, quantitative estimates with values/ranges, excludes literature reviews and meta-analyses.

Screening Strategies:

  • Two-stage AI (abstract → full-text): Recall 0.81
  • Human abstract → AI full-text: Recall 0.92
  • Direct AI full-text: Recall 0.89
e

Data Extraction

0%

Data Extraction

Extraction Categories:

  • Parameters: 47 types incl. R0, CFR, incubation period, serial interval, generation time, delays
  • Models: Model structure, assumptions, interventions simulated
  • Outbreaks: Location, timeline, case counts, demographics

Process: Presence flagging, structured tool calls with schema validation, population context and uncertainty capture.

f

Report Generation

0%

Report Generation

Generation Process: Descriptive statistics and visualizations, evidence tables with traceability to source articles, LLM narrative synthesis.

Self-Refinement (K=5 iterations): Rubric-based critique across 8 dimensions (completeness, clarity, evidence grounding, etc.), targeted revision, verification of claims.

Output Reports:

  • Models review (architecture, interventions)
  • Outbreaks review (timeline, geography)
  • Parameters synthesis (quantitative estimates with uncertainty)

AgentSLR (with GPT-OSS-120B) completes the end-to-end workflow in 20 hours versus 385 hours for manual review — a 19.3× speedup. Running continuously, this corresponds to less than 1 calendar day versus 48.1 human workdays (assuming 8-hour days), yielding 58× calendar-time savings.

Key Findings

  • 58× Calendar-Time Savings: AgentSLR reduces active review time from 48.1 human workdays to less than 1 day.
  • High Screening Recall: Two-stage screening achieves 0.81 recall; direct full-text processing reaches 0.89 without human involvement.
  • Parameter Extraction Ceiling: No model exceeds F1 = 0.63 for parameter extraction, suggesting the bottleneck is task ambiguity, not model capability.
  • Expert Validation: Human experts rate field-level accuracy at 0.80 on average, 18.8 percentage points above automated metrics.
  • Cost-Effective: Open-weight GPT-OSS-120B achieves competitive performance at 96× lower cost than frontier closed-source models.

Implications

  • Human-in-the-Loop: AgentSLR is best deployed with expert oversight — high recall ensures relevant evidence reaches validators.
  • Open-Weight Advantage: Open models enable version pinning and local deployment, critical for reproducible living reviews.
  • Living Reviews: The efficiency gains enable continuous evidence updates as new research emerges.
  • Outbreak Response: Generate preliminary reviews within days during emerging pathogen outbreaks.
  • Resource Democratization: Make systematic review capacity accessible to resource-limited settings.

Future Directions

  • Human Uplift Study: Formal quantification of time savings with human-in-the-loop implementation.
  • Multi-Domain Extension: Adapt pipeline to clinical trials, social science, and other systematic review domains.
  • Heterogeneous Multi-Agent: Route sub-tasks to models with complementary capability profiles.
  • Error Structure Analysis: Distinguish random from systematic failure modes for high-stakes contexts.
  • Cross-Lingual Support: Extend to non-English literature with multilingual models.
